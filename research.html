Research

Spin · charge · light interconversion

We apply synthetic molecular chemistry to solid-state hybrid semiconductors, then engineer them into opto-electronic and energy devices — from displays that emit pure color to catalysts that turn CO2 and water into fuel.

01 — Optoelectronics

Light-emitting diodes & displays

For displays and solid-state lighting, we fabricate efficient light-emitting diodes based on organics (OLEDs) and emerging nanomaterials — perovskites and colloidal nanocrystals.

We are also interested in circularly-polarized-light (CPL) emitting devices, and — to bring the metaverse to life — in modulating the light path from OLEDs to achieve high-resolution near-eye displays. Our perovskite LEDs have reached record external quantum efficiencies and large-area uniformity.

02 — Optoelectronics

Solar cells & photodetectors

The same absorbing materials, turned around — from emitting light to harvesting it — for photovoltaics and light sensors, including bio-inspired photonic synapses.

Using an automation system, we fabricate solution-processed LEDs and photovoltaics at high throughput. Our goal is autonomous optimization of multilayer devices, leveraging artificial intelligence for experiment design and accelerating the discovery loop.

03 — Fundamentals

Chirality & spin

Chiral-induced spin selectivity (CISS): a molecule's handedness controls an electron's spin — enabling a room-temperature spin light-emitting diode.

Using chiral nanomaterials we build CPL detectors and pursue infrared- or CPL-based sensing of biological materials and light communication. The same spin-selective principle extends to catalysis, where spin-polarized reactions can enhance oxygen and hydrogen evolution.

04 — Catalysis

CO2 reduction & water splitting

Closing the carbon and energy loop with photo- and electro-catalysts.

We convert CO2 into CO, CH4, and C2+ products using photocatalysts and electrocatalysts, synthesizing novel catalytic materials and running electrochemical reactions in a variety of cells. For green hydrogen, we design spin-catalysts that facilitate the oxygen/hydrogen evolution reaction, improving anion-exchange-membrane water electrolysis (AEMWE) and fuel cells (AEMFC).

05 — Materials synthesis

Colloidal nanocrystals

We extend our molecular design strategy to the synthesis of luminescent nanocrystals, focusing on ligand and crystal engineering.

By precisely tuning surface ligands, crystal structure, and defect passivation, we improve charge transport and boost light-harvesting and emission efficiencies — the foundation for our brightest, most stable emitters.

06 — Energy storage

Battery electrolytes

Comprehensive extraction and quantitative analysis of lithium-ion battery electrolytes.

We predict side reactions during fabrication and operation, and decode aging mechanisms — revealing, for example, formation-induced electrolyte reactions that shape a cell's whole life.
