About the lab

Molecules to devices, light to energy

The Next-generation Renewable Energy & electronics Laboratory (NREL) designs and synthesizes solid-state hybrid and optical semiconductors — combining the tunable functionality of molecules with the robustness of extended solids.

From these materials we build stacked opto-electronic devices — for optical communication, displays, solar cells, and photosensors — and energy devices for CO2 reduction and hydrogen evolution. We also extract and analyze lithium-ion battery electrolytes to decode side reactions and aging mechanisms. The lab shares its name with the U.S. National Renewable Energy Laboratory, where Prof. Kim carried out his postdoctoral research. Explore our research →

Research at a glance

Six directions, one thread

The fundamentals of spin–charge–light interconversion, applied across optoelectronics, catalysis, and energy materials.

01

Light-emitting diodes & displays

Perovskite, nanocrystal, and organic LEDs for wide-gamut displays and CPL emitters.
02

Solar cells & photodetectors

Harvesting light with the same absorbers — plus robot- and AI-driven high-throughput fabrication.
03

Chirality & spin

Chiral-induced spin selectivity for room-temperature spin-LEDs, CPL detectors, and spin-catalysis.
04

Catalysis — CO2 & H2

Photo- and electro-catalysts that convert CO2 to fuels and split water for green hydrogen.
05

Colloidal nanocrystals

Ligand and crystal engineering of luminescent nanocrystals for brighter, more stable emission.
06

Battery electrolytes

Quantitative extraction and analysis of Li-ion electrolytes to reveal aging mechanisms.
